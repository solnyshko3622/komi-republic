Fire Tower of Syktyvkar

Architectural monument and city symbol

About the Attraction

Built in 1901, the Fire Tower is one of the most recognizable symbols of Syktyvkar. Historically used to spot fires across the wooden city, it now houses a museum dedicated to the history of the fire department. The distinctive octagonal structure and bright colors make it a must-visit landmark for anyone interested in the architectural heritage of the Komi Republic.

Inside, visitors can climb the winding stairs to see historical uniforms, equipment, and photographs dating back to the early 20th century. The top deck, once used by lookouts to survey the city for smoke, offers a unique panoramic view of the surrounding historical district.
